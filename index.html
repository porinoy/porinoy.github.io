01 — Why this corpus exists

Four print eras, one column

The matrimonial column is one of the longest-running, most formally constrained genres in South Asian print classifieds. Its layout has quietly mutated across four decades of typesetting technology — while the underlying task, fitting a life story into the smallest billable space, has not. Scrub through the eras below.

1984 — Dense-packing era. Hand-set hot-metal columns, hairline rules, near-zero whitespace between listings. Ink-bleed and paper aging are common; bounding boxes must be drawn within a 2px tolerance to avoid merging adjacent ads.

1998 — Early phototypesetting. Column counts increase as desktop publishing tools enter newsrooms. Headers remain visually near-identical to Bride listings — the ambiguity that motivates BANDHAN's language-aware refinement.

2010 — Standardized grid. Digital typesetting introduces consistent gutters and grid-aligned columns. Aspect ratios stabilize for headers, while individual ad blocks remain budget-driven and highly variable.

2024 — Digital hybrid layout. Rounded card-like ad blocks, variable typography, and design flourishes appear alongside legacy column conventions — a hybrid of newsprint habit and web-influenced design.

02 — Acquisition

Sourcing & digitization

We performed stratified sampling from six major Bengali newspapers — Ananda Bazar Patrika, Bartaman, Ei Samay, Sangbad Pratidin, Aajkaal, and Ganashakti. Physical copies were digitized at 300 DPI using flatbed scanners.

Sampling was designed to maximize temporal coverage across four print-technology eras, deliberately capturing systematic variation in typesetting convention and document degradation rather than treating it as noise to be discarded.

03 — Annotation pipeline

A human-in-the-loop pipeline for layout and text

Two coupled annotation tasks turn a scanned newspaper page into structured, machine-readable ground truth.

01

Layout segmentation

Spatial regions annotated in LabelMe across three semantic classes: Advertisement, Bride, and Groom. "Advertisement" denotes the cropped matrimonial listing itself; "Bride" / "Groom" denote section headers. Annotators held to a strict 2px boundary tolerance to prevent merge errors in dense, narrow-gutter columns.

COCO JSON format
02

Multi-engine OCR drafting

A custom GUI, 3-Netra ("The Third Eye"), generates baseline transcriptions for each cropped advertisement using three OCR engines — OLM-OCR2, DeepSeek-VL, and EasyOCR — run in parallel for every region.

Python · custom tool
03

Selection & correction

Rather than transcribing from scratch, annotators select the most accurate of the three OCR candidates and edit it to match ground truth — saved as one text file per annotation box. 10% of samples underwent double-blind review by independent linguists.

05 — What makes BANDHAN hard

Dataset characteristics & challenges

BANDHAN's difficulty doesn't come from scale — it comes from the genre's own structure, compared against generic layout corpora such as PubLayNet.

Temporal distribution & class imbalancevs. balanced synthetic sets

Publication volume grows substantially across the four decades. Because a single Bride or Groom header governs a long column of individual listings, Advertisement instances vastly outnumber header classes — forcing models to learn minority-class representations under heavy imbalance.

Bimodal geometric distributionaspect ratio extremes

Bride/Groom headers form a tight, low-variance cluster of short-and-wide boxes reflecting standardized editorial formatting. Advertisement boxes, by contrast, range from small squares to tall columns — driven by cost-per-character pricing rather than semantic content.

Visual similarityheader vs. ad confusion

Bride and Groom headers frequently share identical fonts, sizes, and top-of-column position — geometrically near-indistinguishable from each other, motivating language-aware label refinement rather than geometry alone.

Temporal drift & degradation1980s vs. 2020s

The archive shifts from dense-packing layouts with hairline rules (1980s) to standardized grids (2020s). Early decades carry ink-bleed, non-linear warping, and paper aging — a genuinely rigorous robustness testbed.
